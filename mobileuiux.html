Maritime Psychology & Safety Management Systems

The Human Element Variable in Modern Seafaring

CADET: Anointed Ephraim Okowhaoke | 2024/NSC/HND/035

1. Statement of the Problem

While SOLAS and MARPOL provide the hardware and environmental rules, the "Human Element" remains the most volatile variable. We are witnessing a transition from the traditional camaraderie of the sea to a modern "Cabin Culture."

Cognitive Attrition

Socio-technical isolation causes "micro-lapses" in concentration, leading to a loss of situational awareness during high-traffic maneuvers.

Regulatory Lag

The MLC focuses on calories and hours of rest, but ignores "Social Health," leaving psychological safety unmonitored in current SMS protocols.

2. Case Study: The "Lagos Anchorage" Incident

Location: Lagos Safe Anchorage Area (SAA) / Commodore Pool.
Vessel: 15,000 TEU Container Carrier.
Context: Intense traffic of fishing trawlers, bunker barges, and incoming/outgoing vessels.

The Event: During a night transit through the Lagos pilotage channel, the Second Officer (OOW) fell into a state of "Cognitive Fog." Despite an Able Seaman (AB) being present on the bridge, there was zero verbal communication for 45 minutes. Both were experiencing "Socio-Technical Seclusion," preferring digital isolation over professional bonding.

Outcome: A near-miss with a local fishing vessel was only averted when the Master heard the VHF alarm and intervened. The root cause was not lack of sleep, but Social Detachment causing a breakdown in Bridge Resource Management (BRM).
